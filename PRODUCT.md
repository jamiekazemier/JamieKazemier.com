# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary users are rowers, crews, and families looking for photographs from rowing events. They arrive with a practical goal, to find or buy images from a race, but the site should also make the photography feel intentional, premium, and worth remembering.

Secondary users include clubs or event organizers who may judge whether Jamie Kazemier Photography understands the sport well enough to cover a future race.

## Product Purpose

Jamie Kazemier Photography is a rowing photography portfolio and sales gateway. The site should present rowing images with enough creative force to feel like a distinctive photography brand, while keeping the path to event galleries, buying information, email, and Instagram clear.

Success means visitors quickly understand that Jamie specializes in rowing, feel the work has an artful point of view, and can move from admiration to finding or requesting photos without friction.

## Brand Personality

Creative, premium, made with intent.

The brand should feel visually composed and deliberate rather than decorative. It should have an editorial, artsy quality that makes the photography feel selected and authored, not uploaded into a generic template.

## Anti-references

The site must not feel generic, bland, template-like, or like a default photography grid with swapped-in images. Avoid safe portfolio sameness, overly neutral typography, predictable card grids, stock-gallery polish, and any layout that makes rowing feel like a category label rather than a lived visual subject.

The site should also avoid modal-first image viewing and avoid making the buying flow feel like a dead placeholder.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the photograph as an authored object, not decoration.
2. Make event browsing practical without letting utility flatten the art direction.
3. Use typography, color, and rhythm to create a memorable rowing-specific atmosphere.
4. Keep commercial actions clear: portfolio, buy photos, contact.
5. Prefer intentional asymmetry, pacing, and image scale over generic card repetition.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Follow public-site accessibility basics: strong text contrast, concise descriptive alt text for portfolio images, keyboard-friendly navigation, visible focus states, semantic page structure, and reduced-motion support. Expressive art direction is welcome, but it must not make the site harder to read, navigate, or use on mobile.
